Dad Jokes

The third Sunday in June is Father's Day. That's the day you have to laugh at his jokes. It's time to celebrate Dads and Dad Jokes. Wikipedia describes a dad joke as, "A dad joke is a pun or a question and answer that is often inoffensive and intended to elicit a groan." I describe a Dad joke as funny.

This page was inspired by laughing with family and a recent event at my house. My wife recived an order of fabric, the bag was branded with a pun. "And Sew it begins." Knowing how my son felt about a good pun. I showed it to him. He smacked the shipping bag.
Wife: "You hit my fabric!"
Son: "No, I punched the joke."
Me: "You punched the punchline." He didn't like that joke either.

So let's hear some Joookes! Pulled from the internet.

  • How did Darth Vader know what Luke was getting for his birthday? * He felt his presents.
  • How do you follow Will Smith in the snow? * You follow the fresh prints.
  • Why'd the alternate universe Spider-Man do so well on his driving test? He's an excellent parallel Parker
  • I only seem to get sick on weekdays. I must have a weekend immune system.
  • Of all the inventions of the last 100 years, the dry erase board has to be the most remarkable.
  • This morning, Siri said, “Don’t call me Shirley.” I accidentally left my phone in Airplane mode.
